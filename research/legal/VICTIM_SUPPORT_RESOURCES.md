# Victim Support Resources for NWO Cybermobbing Cases
**Date:** 2026-03-23  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive resource guide for victims of NWO and similar cybermobbing attacks  

## Official Support Organizations

### 1. WEISSER RING e. V.
- **Services:** Nationwide victim support network
- **Coverage:** 420 branches across Germany with 3,000+ volunteers
- **Specialization:** Criminal victim support including cybercrime
- **Contact:** https://weisser-ring.de/mobbing
- **Services Offered:**
  - Legal advice and representation
  - Psychological support
  - Evidence collection guidance
  - Court accompaniment

### 2. Cybermobbing-Hilfe e.V.
- **Focus:** Specialized cyberbullying victim support
- **Services:** Victim assistance and prevention work
- **Website:** https://www.cybermobbing-hilfe.de/
- **Mission:** Support victims and conduct prevention work

### 3. Bündnis gegen Cybermobbing
- **Type:** Coalition of support organizations
- **Resources:** Comprehensive help directory
- **Website:** https://buendnis-gegen-cybermobbing.de/hilfe.html
- **Services:**
  - Organization directory
  - Counseling services
  - Police prevention portal information

## Law Enforcement Resources

### Police Reporting Channels
- **Local Police:** First point of contact for immediate threats
- **State Criminal Police Offices (LKA):** For serious cybercrime cases
- **BKA:** Federal level for organized cybercrime
- **Online Reporting:** Many states offer online reporting portals

### Evidence Collection Guidelines
1. **Screenshot Everything:** Capture all harassment, threats, and messages
2. **Preserve Metadata:** Keep original files with timestamps
3. **Document Timeline:** Maintain chronological log of all incidents
4. **Report to Platforms:** Use platform reporting mechanisms
5. **Legal Consultation:** Contact victim support organizations before proceeding

## Legal Support

### Criminal Law Basis (Germany)
- **§ 129 StGB:** Formation of criminal organizations
- **§ 238 StGB:** Stalking (Nachstellung)
- **§ 202a StGB:** Violation of confidentiality of the word
- **§ 303b StGB:** Computer sabotage
- **§ 263 StGB:** Computer fraud

### Civil Law Options
- **Injunctions:** Court orders to stop harassment
- **Damages:** Compensation for psychological and financial harm
- **Removal Orders:** Forced removal of defamatory content

## Digital Security Measures

### Immediate Actions
1. **Password Changes:** Update all account passwords
2. **Two-Factor Authentication:** Enable on all platforms
3. **Privacy Settings:** Review and strengthen social media privacy
4. **Account Monitoring:** Set up alerts for suspicious activity

### Long-term Protection
- **Regular Security Audits:** Periodic review of digital footprint
- **Professional Security:** Consider cybersecurity services for high-profile cases
- **Platform Cooperation:** Work with social media companies for protection

## Psychological Support

### Mental Health Resources
- **Trauma Counseling:** Specialized therapy for cybercrime victims
- **Support Groups:** Victim peer support networks
- **Crisis Hotlines:** Immediate emotional support services

### Workplace/School Support
- **HR Departments:** Report workplace-related cybermobbing
- **School Authorities:** Educational institution support systems
- **Union Representation:** Workplace protection through unions

## Documentation Template

### Incident Report Checklist
- [ ] Date and time of incident

- [ ] Screenshot evidence captured
- [ ] Usernames/accounts involved
- [ ] Nature of harassment/threat
